Essential Riding Gear

Proper riding gear can reduce injuries, improve comfort, and help riders stay focused in changing road and weather conditions.

Helmet

A full-face helmet protects the head and face better than most other helmet styles. Riders should choose a properly fitted, safety-rated helmet.

Jacket

Motorcycle jackets protect against abrasion and can include armor in the elbows, shoulders, and back for added impact protection.

Gloves

Gloves protect your hands, improve grip, and reduce discomfort from wind and vibration while riding.

Boots

Boots add ankle support, foot protection, and better stability when stopping or maneuvering the motorcycle.

Why Gear Matters

Many newer riders focus first on the motorcycle itself, but riding gear is just as important. Good gear helps protect your body, improves comfort on long rides, and can help you handle different weather conditions more safely.

Understanding Safety Ratings

Not all motorcycle gear offers the same level of protection. Riders should look for gear with recognized safety certifications when choosing helmets, jackets, gloves, pants, and boots.

Gear Type Best Good Basic
Jackets / Pants / Suits AAA AA A
Armor CE Level 2 CE Level 1 Non-rated
Helmets ECE 22.06 / Snell ECE 22.05 DOT only

In general, AAA-rated clothing offers the highest level of abrasion resistance, while CE Level 2 armor provides stronger impact protection than Level 1. For helmets, riders should look for trusted certifications such as ECE 22.06, Snell, or at minimum DOT approval.

Trusted Gear Brands

Many riders choose brands such as Dainese and Alpinestars for jackets, gloves, boots, and other protective gear because they are known for strong materials, racing influence, and high-quality construction. For helmets, brands like Shoei and AGV are widely respected for comfort, design, and advanced safety standards.
